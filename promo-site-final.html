The 10 Medical Traditions

• Western Medicine

Evidence-based diagnostics, pharmacology, and treatment protocols. Includes advanced understanding of pathophysiology, lab value interpretation, imaging analysis, and current medical research. Trained on peer-reviewed studies and clinical guidelines.

• Ayurveda

The 5,000-year-old system of natural healing from India. Constitutional analysis (Vata, Pitta, Kapha), pulse diagnosis, herbal formulations, dietary therapy, lifestyle practices (dinacharya), seasonal routines (ritucharya), and mind-body balance through yoga and meditation.

• Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)

Ancient healing system based on Qi (vital energy), Yin-Yang balance, Five Elements theory, meridian pathways, tongue and pulse diagnosis, acupuncture points, Chinese herbal medicine, dietary therapy, and Qi Gong practices.

• Naturopathy

Focuses on the body's inherent ability to heal itself using natural therapies. Includes clinical nutrition, botanical medicine, homeopathy, hydrotherapy, physical medicine, and lifestyle counseling. Emphasizes treating the root cause and supporting the whole person.

• Functional Medicine

Systems-based approach that addresses root causes of disease through personalized treatment. Advanced testing (hormones, gut health, nutrient status, genetic factors), biochemical pathway analysis, addressing chronic inflammation, optimizing mitochondrial function, and restoring balance.

• Clinical Nutrition

Therapeutic use of food and nutrients to prevent and treat disease. Includes macronutrient optimization, micronutrient therapy, supplementation protocols, elimination diets, anti-inflammatory nutrition, gut healing protocols, and metabolic optimization.

• Herbal Medicine

Plant-based healing using medicinal herbs, tinctures, teas, and botanical preparations. Includes Western herbalism, traditional folk medicine, and pharmacognosy. Understanding of active compounds, herb-drug interactions, contraindications, and evidence-based herbal protocols.

• Chiropractic

Focus on spinal health, nervous system function, and musculoskeletal alignment. Includes spinal adjustments, joint mobilization, postural analysis, ergonomic guidance, and understanding the connection between structural alignment and overall health.

• Physical Therapy

Movement-based healing and rehabilitation. Includes injury recovery protocols, manual therapy techniques, therapeutic exercise, gait analysis, pain management strategies, mobility restoration, and functional movement optimization.

• Fitness & Exercise Science

Science-based training principles for strength, endurance, flexibility, and performance. Includes program design, progressive overload, periodization, recovery optimization, injury prevention, and understanding how exercise impacts overall health and longevity.
